## 2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to:

- Make websites work or work more efficiently
- Remember your preferences and settings
- Provide analytics about how the website is used
- Enable personalized experiences

**Similar Technologies:** We may also use similar technologies such as:
- **Web Beacons / Pixel Tags**: Small graphic images that track user behavior
- **Local Storage**: Browser-based storage for offline data
- **Session Storage**: Temporary data that is cleared when the browser is closed
- **IndexedDB**: Browser-based database for structured data

### 3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the Service to function and cannot be switched off. They are set in response to actions you take, such as logging in or filling out forms.

| Cookie Name          | Purpose                              | Duration      |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------- |

**Legal Basis:** These cookies do not require consent as they are strictly necessary for service operation.

## 6. How to Control Cookies

### 6.1 Browser Settings

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings:

- **Chrome**: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
- **Firefox**: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
- **Safari**: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
- **Edge**: Settings → Cookies and site permissions
- **Opera**: Settings → Advanced → Privacy and security → Cookies

### 6.2 Opt-Out Tools

- **Google Analytics Opt-Out**: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
- **Network Advertising Initiative**: https://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/
- **Digital Advertising Alliance**: https://www.aboutads.info/choices/
- **European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance**: https://www.youronlinechoices.eu/

### 6.3 Cookie Consent Manager

You can update your cookie preferences at any time using the cookie consent banner or settings in the Service.

### 6.4 Do Not Track

We respect the "Do Not Track" (DNT) browser signal. When DNT is enabled, we will not set analytics or marketing cookies.
